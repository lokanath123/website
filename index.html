About Me


  • I am a research engineer at the Georgia Institute of Technology, specializing in first-principles modeling of magnetic and magnetoelectric multiferroic materials. With a Ph.D. in Physics from the Central University of Tamil Nadu, India, my research focuses on understanding the properties of materials at the micro- and nanoscale using density functional theory (DFT) to advance sustainable energy technologies.
  • Over the years, I have worked on modeling the mechanical stability of 2D materials, developing computational techniques for solid-state magnetic refrigeration, and training machine learning potentials for Si-Ge systems, including interfaces and amorphous structures. I am passionate about bridging fundamental research with real-world applications, contributing to the discovery and optimization of novel materials for energy-efficient technologies.
